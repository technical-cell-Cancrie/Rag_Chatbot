# LEAD ACID BATTERY INSTRUCTIONS

Dosage:

* add Cancrie Nanocarbon at 0.3% by weight of oxide
* for every 100 kg negative oxide, add 300 g Cancrie Nanocarbon

Paste mixing process:

1. add Cancrie Nanocarbon directly to oxide before adding water and sulfuric acid
2. do not add carbon black, graphite, or other carbon materials
3. add other additives as per existing recipe
4. dry mix additives with oxide for 15 minutes until uniform
5. add 10–12% more water compared to regular carbon black batches
6. add sulfuric acid as per existing recipe
7. add slightly more water later during pasting if required
8. wet/cured negatives should be 3–5% lighter than regular carbon plates

Expected process improvements:

* 75% better negative plate strength
* 5% oxide saving
* 100% saving in carbon black/graphite
* higher Ah/Wh efficiency
* improved high-rate charge/discharge performance
* 20% better charge acceptance
* 20% longer cycle life

Short validation tests:

* plate drop test: expect 50–75% improvement
* charge acceptance test at 14.4V for 12V battery:

  * 25–60% improvement in SLI batteries
  * 20% improvement in tubular batteries

Flat-plate battery benefits:

* approximately 5% lower plate weight
* up to 60% higher charge acceptance
* up to 60% better plate strength
* up to 15% better PSOC cycling performance

Flat-plate operational notes:

* CCA initially remains same and improves with cycling
* HRD remains same
* water loss remains same

Tubular battery benefits:

* approximately 4% lower plate weight
* Ah/Wh efficiency increases from ~92–93% to ~96–97%
* up to 8% improvement in C5/C3/C2/C1 capacities
* up to 25% higher charge acceptance
* supports charging up to 0.2C–0.22C
* up to 40% lower drop-test weight loss
* approximately 20% lifecycle improvement

Tubular battery operational notes:

* C20 to C10 capacity may initially be slightly lower
* capacity stabilizes within 20–25 cycles
* ROC characteristics remain same
* water loss remains same

# FAQ

Q: What is Cancrie Nanocarbon?
A:
Cancrie Nanocarbon is a patented biomass-derived advanced nanocarbon additive for batteries and energy storage systems.

Q: Which battery types are supported?
A:
Lead-acid, lithium-ion, LFP, sodium-ion, LIC, supercapacitors, redox flow batteries, and fuel cells.

Q: Is Cancrie Nanocarbon a drop-in additive?
A:
Yes. Existing manufacturing lines can use the material without major modifications.

Q: Does the process require carbon black or graphite?
A:
No. Carbon black and graphite should not be added with Cancrie Nanocarbon.

Q: What is the recommended dosage?
A:
0.3% by weight of oxide.

Q: What are the main performance gains?
A:
Improved charge acceptance, improved cycle life, lower resistance, reduced sulfation, improved strength, and faster charging.

Q: Is the technology validated?
A:
Yes. Validation includes ARAI, CES, NABL labs, and international accredited laboratories.

Q: Is the material suitable for high-temperature environments?
A:
Yes. The material improves thermal stability and does not significantly increase water loss.

Q: Does Cancrie Nanocarbon support fast charging?
A:
Yes. Stable charging up to 0.22C has been demonstrated.

Q: Is the material sustainable?
A:
Yes. The material is biomass-derived and manufactured using lower-energy processes.
